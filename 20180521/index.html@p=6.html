About

As a professor in Health Informatics, I certainly feel the pain and suffering of patients and their families when I hear about their medical experiences. So I built this website to share my knowledge and information about the massive and complex healthcare system in the United States. My ultimate goal is for the public to become more informed of the healthcare industry and obtain access to quality care with minimal financial burden, which requires transparent medical care.

The significance of healthcare transparency cannot be overemphasized. It is one of the most important factors in determining the success or failure of the ongoing healthcare reform in the United States. Ideally, the reform should ensure that transparency be imbibed by all health workers and government officials, together with appropriate communication and education of the common citizens. Below are some of the most concrete reasons transparency must be the watchword of US healthcare policy:

  • Accountability

From emerging evidences, transparency encourages accountability by holding all stakeholders and healthcare regulators accountable for their actions and failures. In the very complex healthcare system with multiple stakeholders (e.g., patients, providers, insurance company, employers, regulatory agencies and public policy makers), quite often what happens is that no single party could understand why unexpected bad things happen and how to trace the accountability. This shows that without transparency, accountability has proven to be a system failure.

  • Choice

Transparency in healthcare delivery gives the populace the option of choice. In a transparent health system, the citizens can make informed decisions, when switching between medical plans, selecting providers, or making a critical medical decision. Each one of us unavoidably becomes the consumer of health services at some time of our life. It would be extremely helpful if making informed medical decision could be as simple as ordering food in a restaurant, or if not close, becomes SIMPLER.

  • Productivity

Information sharing and transparency is the key for the “invisible hand” of the market and economics principles to play their magic for economic efficiency and maximal societal benefit. When there is transparency in the publication of data and performance sheets, not only practitioners and healthcare workers, but also insurance companies and other stakeholders will tend to be more committed to deliver good quality healthcare services. Productivity comes from a fair game with clear accountability and aligned incentives.

Having said these, healthcare transparency goes a long way probably with both reforms and disruptive innovations.
