Biography:

Dr. Lixia Yao was an assistant professor in the health informatics lab from 2013 to 2016 before moving to Mayo Clinic (Rochester, MN). Previously, she worked as a scientific investigator at GlaxoSmithKline pharmaceuticals. She received a master degree in Computational Sciences from the National University of Singapore in 2004 and a Ph.D. degree in Biomedical Informatics from Columbia University in 2010. Her research and teaching interests are in the broad areas of health informatics. Primarily her research focuses on applying data mining, text mining and data integration techniques on top of various medical data, including electronic health records, scientific literature and social media data to address real world problems in healthcare.

Degree: Ph.D. in Biomedical Informatics, Columbia University, New York (2010)

Biography:

Ming Huang received the Ph.D. degree in Scientific Computation from the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities, Minneapolis, MN, USA in 2014, and the dual B.Sc. degrees in Computer Science and Applied Chemistry from Beijing Normal University, Beijing, China in 2004. From 2014 to 2017, he worked as a Postdoctoral Associate in the Institute of Quantitative Biomedicine, Rutgers University for the development and application of computational models for biological catalysis. Since 2017, he has been a Research Associate with the Division of Biomedical Statistics and Informatics, Health Sciences Department in Mayo Clinic. His current research interests include machine learning and medical data mining.

Degree: Ph.D. in Scientific Computation, University of Minnesota-Twin Cities(2014)
