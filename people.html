Lixia Yao, Ph.D.
Assistant Professor
Email: Yao.Lixia At Mayo DoT edu

Biography:
Lixia Yao, PhD, is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Health Sciences Research at Mayo Clinic. She received her Ph.D. degree in Biomedical Informatics from Columbia University in 2010. She then worked as a Principal Investigator at GlaxoSmithKline Pharmaceuticals and the University of North Carolina at Charlotte as an assistant professor prior to Mayo. Her research focuses on mining, integrating, and transforming unstructured, incomplete, and noisy data (i.e., electronic health records and claims databases, literature, patents, and social media) into meaningful biomedical knowledge and informatics applications. By leading a team of data scientists and healthcare professionals at the Health Informatics Lab, and by collaborating with world-class scholars and clinicians, she has published in several high-profile journals including Nature Biotechnology and Genome Research. She is also the recipient of Career Development Award in Biomedical Informatics from the National Library of Medicine for 2016-2019.

Degree: Ph.D. in Biomedical Informatics, Columbia University, New York (2010)

Maryam Zolnoori, Ph. D.
Research Fellow
Email: Zolnoori.Maryam At Mayo DoT edu

Biography:

Dr. Maryam Zolnoori received her Ph.D. degree in Health Informatics from the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee in 2017, and her first master in Biomedical Informatics from the University of Indiana in 2014, and her second master in Information Technology from the of Tarbiat Modares University, Tehran, Iran. Dr. Zolnoori’s long-term research goal focuses on integrating and mining data sources from electronic health records, consumer health posts, and biomedical literature for improving medications non-adherence, pharmacovigilance, and mental healthcare. She is also interested in developing interventions to support and promote patients’ engagement and patient-centered care. As a pre-doctoral training at the National Library of Medicine (NLM) (2016-2017), she focused on utilizing consumer health posts for medications non-adherence in patients with depression. In 2018, she joined Mayo Clinic as a Research Fellow with Dr. Yao in the Division of Biomedical Statistics and Informatics, Department of health Sciences research.

Degree: Ph.D. in Health Informatics, University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee (2017)
